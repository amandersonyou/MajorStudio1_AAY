YOSEMITE

Yosemite National Park is one of the most beautiful places on Earth and has attracted visitors to its stunning mountainous landscape for centuries. Many works of art featuring Yosemite exist within The Metropolitan Museum of Art's vast collection. This visualization explores two angles of two mediums relating to the MET's Yosemite pieces.

Get Your Bearings

Reference this map from the National Park Service in combination with photographs by Carleton E. Watkins from the MET's collection. Each of the eight selected photographs represents a top tourist destination within the park. The images are aligned to coordinate geographically with the locations along the map. Contour lines have been added along the mountain tops to draw attention to the unique shapes of each natural structure, as well as to communicate the overall land-to-sky composition within each photograph. The contour lines connect between each image to lead the eye across all mountain tops.

Nature in Comparison

Click, hold and drag the green circle across each image to reveal either the historic landscape painting from the MET on the left, or a modern day digital photograph of the same location on the right. Use this visual to evaluate how the painter may have seen these landmark locations when creating these works of art. Compare the use of color and light as well as vantage point.
